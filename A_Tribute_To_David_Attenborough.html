Sir David Attenborough

Broadcaster, Naturalist, Documentarian, and My All-Time Hero

Sir David Attenborough holding a poison dart frog for the camera. (Photo Credit: Ben Dowell, 2013)

Snapshot

Sir David Attenborough, in full Sir David Frederick Attenborough (born May 8, 1926, London, Eng.) English broadcaster and writer noted for his innovative educational programs on television.

He is also a former senior manager at the BBC, having served as controller of BBC Two and director of programming for BBC Television in the 1960s and 1970s. He is the only person to have won BAFTAs for programmes in each of black and white, colour, HD, and 3D.

Attenborough is widely considered a national treasure in Britain, although he himself does not like the term. In 2002 he was named among the 100 Greatest Britons following a UK-wide vote. He is the younger brother of director, producer and actor Richard Attenborough, and older brother of motor executive John Attenborough.
